About

Research profile

I am a postdoctoral associate at NYU Abu Dhabi. My work focuses on reliable decision-making and control for robotics, combining tools from optimization, uncertainty-aware learning, inverse optimal control, and robust planning.

I am particularly interested in how principled learning methods can support embodied intelligence in real robotic systems, including manipulation, model-based control, and safety-aware autonomy.

Research

Current interests

Learning-based control

Model-based and uncertainty-aware methods for nonlinear decision-making and control.

Inverse optimal control

Data-driven approaches for identifying objectives and policies from demonstrations and interaction.

Distributionally robust planning

Robust formulations for planning and control under epistemic uncertainty and ambiguity.

Embodied intelligence

Bridging principled control methods with practical robotic manipulation and autonomous behavior.

Formal guarantees

Safety, robustness, and structure-aware control for reliable deployment in robotics.

Soft robotics

Learning-driven control with formal reasoning for compliant and soft robotic systems.
